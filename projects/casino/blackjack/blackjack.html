Blackjack Rules

Introduction

Blackjack is a popular card game played in casinos around the world.
The objective of the game is to beat the dealer by having a hand value closer to 21 without going over.

Basic Gameplay

- Each player is dealt two cards, and the dealer also receives two cards.

- Number cards are worth their face value, face cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks) are worth 10, and Aces can be worth either 1 or 11, depending on the player's choice.

- Players can choose to "hit" (take another card) or "stand" (keep their current hand) to get as close to 21 as
possible without going over.

- If a player's hand exceeds 21, they "bust" and lose the game. The dealer wins automatically if the player busts.

Winning and Payout

- If a player's hand is closer to 21 than the dealer's without busting, they win the game and are paid 1:1 on their bet.

- If a player is dealt a "Blackjack," which is an Ace and a 10-value card, they win 3:2 on their bet.

- If the player and dealer have the same hand value, it's a "push," and the player's bet is returned.

Conclusion

Blackjack is a game of skill and strategy. By making the right decisions, players can improve their chances of winning and enjoy an exciting casino experience.
